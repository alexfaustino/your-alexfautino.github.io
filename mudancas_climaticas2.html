Europe and Africa

Kenya

The Republic of Kenya is a country in Eastern Africa with a million people of its population left on the brink of famine. Rainfall is erratic which causes more and longer droughts. Global warming highlights the hunger crisis, Kenya is getting drier and hotter causing more than 650,000 children under age 5 to be severely malnourished. The severe heat and lack of rain makes food production impossible, the plants and tree’s seeds are dry and the animals thinner. 12 million people rely on food aid.

Americas

Amazon, Brazil

The Amazon rainforest is the most broadleaf tropical rainforest in the Amazon biome that covers most of the Amazon basin of South America; it represents over half of the planet’s remaining rainforests. For most of human history, deforestation in the Amazon was primarily the product of subsistence farmers who cut down trees for the survival of their families and local consumption. However, the level of deforestation has increased due to industrial activities and large-scale agriculture. Deforestation in the Brazilian Amazon rose more than 88% in June 2019 caused by the 72,843 fires from that same year. Over the last decade, the biggest tropical forest in the world released almost 20% more carbon dioxide than it absorbed. It's becoming the biggest source of CO2 , causing the gas emissions to increase by 50% in the last 50 years. Regardless of these higher levels, the forests were responsible for absorbing 30% of CO2. These same higher levels have proven that degradation is causing more emissions than deforestation whose increase came from the fires of 2019. These extreme events are putting the natural habitat of 45 thousand plants and animals species in danger; the biodiversity is disappearing with this forest.

Artic

The Arctic is a polar region located at the northernmost part of Earth and it is seen as a leading indicator of global warming. This crisis affects the whole planet with temperatures getting higher, even in Arctic with the loss of sea ice and melting Greenland ice sheet. A small increase in temperature can lead to greater warming over time, making the polar regions the most sensitive areas to climate change on Earth. If Arctic ice melts, sea level would rise 70 meters, covering all the coastal cities and land area would shrink significantly.

Asia and Oceania

Japan

Japan is an archipelago in East Asia, located in the northwest Pacific Ocean; it’s one of the most densely populated and urbanized countries. This eleventh-most populous country is the most threatened country in the world by climate change. The biggest effect is the extreme heat waves; the annual temperature has increased 1.0ºC during the last century and it keeps getting warmer. Japan has been experiencing higher temperatures surpassing 35ºC and less extreme cold. This climate crisis’s consequence has changed the levels of snow and precipitation. While its levels didn’t change during the 20th century, it’s variability like the timing and quantity have been changing leading to unexpected rain patterns which impairs agriculture’s cycles and the management of water resources; Japan had 22 hurricanes with loss of lives and material goods in just one year, 2004. Weather events themselves were behind 1,282 deaths on the island, 1-01 per 100.000 inhabitants and caused economic losses of 35,839 million dollars. While the temperatures have increased in the last 100 years, the ocean levels have also been rising. These heavy rains and heat have as direct consequences faster aging and more infectious diseases putting public health at risk.

The Philippines

The Republic of Philippines is an archipelagic country in Southeast Asia, one of the most typhoon impacted places. Consisting in 7,640 islands, not only is its sea surface temperatures are getting higher, as well as the sea temperatures, factors which lead to stronger typhoons while the sea level rise aggravates these extreme weather events from which The Philippines’s rapidly growing population is vulnerable to. In 2020, The Philippines suffered 3 major storms in less than a month leaving thousands of people homeless and serious floods increasing the risk of landslides as well as rough seas. The Filipino people are affected by 20 storms every year.

Australia

Officially Commonwealth of Australia, Australia is a sovereign country comprising the mainland of the Australian continent, the island of Tasmania and numerous smaller islands. The largest country in Oceania faces a major issue: extinction of several species of biodiversity. Australia faces intense droughts and bushfires from climate change. As the temperatures get warmer, the pollution increases, destroying the habitat of species that are going extinct. However, the main factor is the increasing rate of population growth. This has led to habitat change through land clearing and urbanisation, hunting and exploitation for human consumption and use. In the beginning of 2021, the Australian government officially acknowledged the extinction of 13 endemic species. The country has lost more than 50 animal and 60 plant species in the past 200 years and recorded the highest rate of mammalian extinction in the world over that period.
